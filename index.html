About the Maranao People

The Maranao, meaning "People of the Lake," are one of the largest Muslim ethnic groups in the Philippines, primarily residing around Lake Lanao in Lanao del Sur, Mindanao. Known for their rich Islamic heritage, intricate okir art, and the iconic Torogan houses, the Maranao have preserved their unique culture and traditions for centuries.

Traditional Clothing

The Maranao are known for their vibrant traditional attire, including the malong (tubular garment), elaborate Muslim dress, and colorful accessories. Their clothing reflects Islamic influence and indigenous artistic traditions.

Location

The Maranao primarily inhabit the areas surrounding Lake Lanao in Lanao del Sur, Mindanao. The lake is central to their identity, livelihood, and cultural practices.

Architecture

The Torogan is a traditional Maranao house, elevated on posts with elaborate okir carvings. These ancestral homes symbolize status and showcase the exceptional woodcarving skills of Maranao artisans.

Where They Live

The Maranao people primarily inhabit the provinces surrounding Lake Lanao in the Lanao region of Mindanao, Philippines. Their name "Maranao" literally translates to "People of the Lake," reflecting their deep connection to this body of water.

Lanao del Sur

The majority of Maranao people live in Lanao del Sur province, with major population centers in Marawi City (the Islamic City of Marawi), Taraka, Marantao, and surrounding municipalities around Lake Lanao.

Marawi City

Known as the "Islamic City of the Philippines," Marawi City serves as the cultural and economic center of the Maranao people. It is home to beautiful mosques, the Mindanao State University, and traditional Maranao architecture.

Lake Lanao

Lake Lanao is the second largest lake in the Philippines and the heart of Maranao territory. At 780 feet above sea level, it provides water, transportation, and sustenance to the Maranao communities surrounding it.

Lanao del Norte

Some Maranao communities also reside in Lanao del Norte province, particularly in municipalities near the lake's northern shores, maintaining cultural ties with their Lanao del Sur relatives.

Other Settlements

Maranao diaspora communities can be found throughout Mindanao and other parts of the Philippines, particularly in urban centers, though they maintain strong cultural and family connections to their ancestral homeland.

Geographic Features

Geographic Features

The Maranao territory is characterized by mountainous terrain, lush vegetation, and the dominant presence of Lake Lanao. The climate is tropical, with abundant rainfall supporting agriculture and the lake ecosystem.

Cultural Practices

Traditional Dances

The Maranao are famous for the Singkil dance, performed with bamboo poles and fans, depicting royal courtship. Other traditional dances include the Sagayan, a war dance showcasing martial prowess.

Okir Art

Okir is the distinctive Maranao art form featuring intricate curvilinear designs and motifs. This woodcarving and metalwork tradition adorns everything from houses to musical instruments and boats.

Islamic Faith

Islam is central to Maranao identity and culture. The Maranao follow Islamic teachings while maintaining unique local traditions, creating a rich blend of religious and indigenous practices.

Cuisine

Maranao cuisine features rice, fish from Lake Lanao, and dishes influenced by Islamic dietary laws. Popular dishes include piyaparan (fish in spicy coconut sauce) and various rice-based delicacies.

Music

The kulintang ensemble is central to Maranao music, consisting of bronze gongs arranged in a row. This ancient musical tradition is used in ceremonies, celebrations, and cultural performances.

Darangen Epic

The Darangen is a Maranao epic poem and oral tradition recognized by UNESCO. It narrates the adventures of legendary heroes and embodies Maranao values, history, and cultural wisdom.

Historical Timeline

Pre-Islamic Era

Before the arrival of Islam, the Maranao practiced animistic beliefs and developed sophisticated metalworking and boat-building traditions around Lake Lanao.

Islamization (13th-14th Century)

Islam arrived through Arab and Malay traders and missionaries. The Maranao embraced Islam, which became integral to their identity while preserving indigenous cultural elements.

Sultanate Period

The Maranao established powerful sultanates around Lake Lanao, including the Sultanate of Lanao. These political structures governed Maranao society and resisted colonial powers.

Colonial Resistance

The Maranao fiercely resisted Spanish and American colonization, maintaining autonomy longer than most Philippine groups. Their resistance is a source of pride and cultural identity.

Modern Era

Today, the Maranao continue to preserve their Islamic faith, artistic traditions, and cultural heritage. They play a significant role in Mindanao's cultural landscape and Philippine Muslim society.
